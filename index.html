About Bourbon Hunter

Crafted by enthusiasts, refined by data

verified Built on real tasting tables, not marketing decks.

Bourbon Hunter began as a shared spreadsheet between friends in Louisville who were tired of confusing scores, recycled tasting notes, and missed allocations. What started as a personal project — a way to track bottles, blind-tasting results, and honest impressions — grew into a dedicated platform combining expert panel reviews, structured data, and modern tooling like Claude from Anthropic. Today, Bourbon Hunter serves enthusiasts, bar programs, and clubs across the country, helping them move beyond hype and discover bottles that genuinely deserve a spot on the shelf.

Our mission is simple: make great bourbon easier to find, understand, and enjoy. We believe in transparency over pay‑to‑play lists, curiosity over snobbery, and education over gatekeeping. Every feature we ship is guided by three core values — honesty, craftsmanship, and community. Bourbon Hunter exists to empower drinkers at every level to explore with confidence, share what they love, and build collections that reflect their unique palate, not someone else’s trending list.

FAQ

Answers to common questions

Learn how Bourbon Hunter works, how we score bottles, and what to expect as you start your tasting journey.

Our scoring is driven by a structured tasting framework used by an expert panel, not by advertising budgets or brand sponsorships. Each bottle is blind‑tasted when possible and evaluated across aroma, palate, mouthfeel, finish, balance, and overall distinctiveness. Those criteria are combined into a 100‑point score with detailed notes explaining why a bottle rated where it did. We also overlay a value‑to‑price score to highlight bottles that punch above their weight, even if they aren’t the rarest or most hyped releases.
When you first join, we ask a few quick questions about what you already enjoy — including flavor notes (sweet, spicy, fruity, oaky), proof range, and typical price comfort zone. From there, Bourbon Hunter, powered by Claude from Anthropic, compares your stated preferences and any ratings you give to a large dataset of expert tasting notes and community feedback. Over time, the system learns which flavor clusters you consistently rate highly and which you avoid, allowing us to surface bourbons that are statistically more likely to hit your sweet spot while still nudging you toward thoughtful, interesting discoveries.
Bourbon Hunter is focused first and foremost on helping you decide what to hunt, not operating as a marketplace. We track availability indicators, release patterns, and general distribution levels to give you a realistic sense of how difficult a bottle may be to find. In some regions, we may surface links to licensed retail partners, but those connections never influence our ratings or recommendations. You’re always free to purchase wherever you prefer — our goal is to send you into the market with a clear, confident shortlist.
Bourbon Hunter is intentionally built for both curious newcomers and seasoned collectors. If you’re newer to bourbon, you’ll find friendly, jargon‑free tasting notes, guided flights, and clear starting points at approachable price ranges. If you’re more advanced, you can dig into nuanced ratings, rare release tracking, and deeper comparisons between distilleries, mash bills, and single barrel picks. Either way, our goal is to respect your time and budget while making your next pour more rewarding.
We take privacy seriously. Your personal information and tasting history are used solely to improve your recommendations and the overall performance of the platform. Individual ratings are not publicly attributed to you unless you explicitly choose to share them (for example, in a club or group context). Aggregate, anonymized data may be used to better understand flavor trends and improve our scoring models, but your identity and specific activity remain protected.
